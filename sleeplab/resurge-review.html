Resurge Review — 30 Nights with a Sleep Tracker

Affiliate Disclosure: SleepLab is an independent research site. Some links are affiliate links. We may earn a commission if you purchase, at no extra cost to you.

I tracked sleep with a wearable for 30 nights — first 7 days baseline, then 23 days with Resurge. Here's what the data actually showed, not what the product page claims.

Why I Tested Resurge

I'd already built and followed a behavioral sleep protocol for 3 weeks (consistent wake time, 66°F bedroom, blue light blocking, 1pm caffeine cutoff). My sleep improved — but deep sleep duration was still low at around 42 minutes per night. The research suggests 60–90 minutes is optimal for HGH release and metabolic repair.

Resurge's ingredient stack targets the hormonal side of sleep specifically: melatonin for initiation, ashwagandha for cortisol reduction, L-theanine for nervous system calming, and arginine + lysine as HGH precursors. This is the gap my behavioral protocol wasn't filling.

Ingredient Analysis

Melatonin (10mg): High dose — effective for sleep initiation and resetting circadian rhythm. Some people are sensitive at this dose. I wasn't.

Ashwagandha (150mg): KSM-66 extract. Strong evidence for cortisol reduction and subjective sleep quality improvement. This was likely driving the deep sleep increase.

L-Theanine (200mg): Well-studied for promoting relaxed alertness and improving sleep architecture without sedation. Standard dose.

L-Arginine + L-Lysine (1200mg each): HGH precursors. Evidence for stimulating GH release during sleep, particularly in older adults. Interesting inclusion in a weight-focused sleep supplement.

Magnesium + Zinc: Both involved in sleep architecture. Deficiency in either impairs sleep quality — common in adults over 40.

30-Night Tracker Data

Baseline (nights 1–7): Deep sleep avg 41 min/night. REM 68 min/night. HRV morning avg 42ms. Sleep efficiency 81%.

Week 2 (nights 8–14): Deep sleep avg 48 min (+17%). HRV 44ms. REM essentially unchanged.

Week 3 (nights 15–21): Deep sleep avg 58 min (+41% vs baseline). This was the clear inflection point. Morning energy noticeably different.

Week 4 (nights 22–30): Deep sleep avg 63 min. HRV 49ms (+17% vs baseline). Sleep efficiency 88%. Nighttime waking reduced from 2–3× to 0–1× per night.

Weight change over 30 days: -4.2 lbs without any deliberate diet change. I attribute this primarily to improved sleep quality and reduced overnight cortisol.

Honest Verdict

Resurge meaningfully improved deep sleep duration for me — 41 to 63 minutes over 30 nights. The weight change was a secondary benefit, not the primary mechanism I was testing. Both were real.

It's not cheap for ongoing use. The 180-day guarantee makes it worth testing properly. I'd recommend running it for at least 4 weeks before evaluating — the effect built progressively, not overnight.
