People dealing with acne or breakout-prone skin might benefit from sonic cleansing brushes, as results may vary depending on acne severity and underlying causes. Users with Combination Skin

Combination skin types—where some areas are oily and others are dry—may experience balanced benefits from sonic cleansing brushes. Results may vary across different facial zones, with potentially better results in oily areas and possible dryness in sensitive regions. Users with combination skin should adjust settings accordingly and monitor how different areas respond, as results may vary significantly between the T-zone and cheeks.

Those Seeking Enhanced Skincare Results

Who Should Avoid or Exercise Caution

People with Sensitive Skin

Those with naturally sensitive or easily irritated skin should approach sonic cleansing brushes cautiously, as results may vary and irritation risks exist. These individuals may experience redness, stinging, or increased sensitivity, though results may differ based on brush brand and intensity settings. If considering use, starting with the gentlest setting and minimal frequency is recommended, and results may vary significantly from standard users.

Individuals with Active Skin Conditions

People with rosacea, eczema, psoriasis, or other active dermatological conditions should consult healthcare providers before using sonic cleansing brushes, as results may vary unpredictably and could potentially worsen symptoms. Individuals who have recently undergone chemical peels, microdermabrasion, laser treatments, or other professional skin procedures should wait before using sonic cleansing brushes, as results may vary based on healing progress.
