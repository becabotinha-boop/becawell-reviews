Is Commission Hero Worth It? An Honest Look at Robby Blanchard's Course

Short answer: Commission Hero can be worth it — but only for a specific type of person willing to invest real money in paid ads upfront. For total beginners with limited budgets, results may vary significantly, and the risks are real. For those serious about Facebook-to-ClickBank affiliate marketing and willing to spend $300–$500/month on ad testing, it provides a genuinely structured path forward.

What Is Commission Hero?

Commission Hero is an online training course created by Robby Blanchard, a ClickBank affiliate marketer who reportedly became the #1 ClickBank affiliate globally. The course teaches a specific method: running Facebook and Instagram ads that send traffic to simple landing pages, which then redirect to ClickBank affiliate offers — primarily in health, weight loss, and similar high-commission niches.

The core promise is a system for earning affiliate commissions through paid traffic, without needing a blog, email list, or SEO presence. That's an appealing premise — though, as with any marketing course, results may vary based on your execution, niche selection, and advertising budget.

What You Actually Get Inside

The course covers three main pillars that most students find genuinely useful:

  • Facebook Ads Setup: Step-by-step guidance on creating and structuring ad campaigns, targeting audiences, and managing spend. This section is considered one of the stronger parts of the course, though Facebook's ad platform changes frequently — some content may be slightly outdated depending on when you access it.
  • Landing Page Strategy: Templates and guidance on building simple one-page "bridge" funnels. The goal is a fast-loading, distraction-free page that pre-sells the ClickBank offer before the click. This approach can work well, though testing may be required before finding a page variation that converts for your audience.
  • Offer Selection: How to identify and evaluate ClickBank products with strong gravity scores and affiliate support. This is genuinely valuable — picking the wrong offer is one of the most common mistakes new affiliates make.

Students also get access to a private Facebook community, which many find helpful for troubleshooting and motivation, and reportedly some access to Robby's top-performing ad creatives.

The Real Cost — Beyond the Course Fee

This is where honest qualifying language matters most. The course itself costs around $997 (with payment plans available). However, that is arguably the smaller part of your real investment.

To follow the Commission Hero method, you'll need an ad budget. Realistically, expect to spend at least $300–$1,000 per month on Facebook ads during the testing phase before finding a profitable campaign — and results may vary substantially. Some students report finding a winning campaign within weeks. Others spend several months and thousands of dollars before hitting profitability, if they do at all.

Additional costs to factor in:

  • Landing page hosting/builder (ClickFunnels or similar: ~$97–$297/month)
  • Domain and email autoresponder if you choose to build a list
  • Stock images or ad creative tools

Total first-year investment — course plus ads plus tools — could realistically land between $3,000 and $6,000 or more. Anyone considering Commission Hero should budget accordingly. This is not a low-cost-entry model.

Who Is Likely to Get the Most Out of It?

Based on available student feedback and the nature of the method, Commission Hero appears most suitable for:

  • People with at least $500–$1,000 available specifically for ad testing — not emergency savings
  • Those who are comfortable with a learning curve on Facebook Ads Manager
  • Individuals who can tolerate early losses while optimizing campaigns
  • Anyone already familiar with digital marketing basics who wants a specific paid-traffic framework

It may be less suitable for complete beginners with no marketing background, anyone with a very tight budget, or people expecting passive income quickly. Results may vary considerably based on the niche chosen, ad creative quality, and how consistently students apply the material.

What Students Say

Testimonials on the sales page feature significant income claims — some students reportedly earning thousands per month. These should be read with appropriate skepticism: highlighted success stories tend to represent outlier results, not the typical experience. Results may vary, and most affiliates who attempt paid traffic models do not achieve the top-tier outcomes featured in marketing materials.

More balanced community feedback suggests that students who do put in consistent effort, treat early ad spend as tuition, and iterate on their campaigns tend to make measurable progress. Those who expect quick passive income often feel disappointed.

The Bottom Line

Commission Hero is a legitimate, structured course teaching a real affiliate marketing method used by serious marketers. The instruction quality is generally solid, the community is active, and the framework — paid traffic to affiliate offers — does work in the right hands.

However, it demands real financial investment beyond the course price, a tolerance for testing and failure, and consistent effort over months — not days. Results may vary based on individual skill, budget, market conditions, and niche selection.

If you go in with realistic expectations and a proper ad budget, Commission Hero can be a worthwhile investment. If you're expecting a done-for-you income stream on a tight budget, you may want to explore lower-cost organic traffic methods first.
