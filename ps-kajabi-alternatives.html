Frequently Asked Questions

Is Kajabi worth the price compared to cheaper alternatives?

For creators who are actively generating revenue — or have a clear, validated offer ready to scale — Kajabi is typically worth the investment. The real cost comparison isn't just the platform fee; it's the time and money saved by not stitching together separate tools for email marketing, course hosting, landing pages, and checkout. Many creators using cheaper alternatives end up paying for three or four additional tools that, combined, exceed Kajabi's price anyway. That said, if you're still in the validation phase and haven't made consistent sales, the cost can be a burden. The smarter approach is to start lean, validate your offer on a free or low-cost platform, and migrate to Kajabi once you have proof of concept. Individual results will vary, and the "worth it" equation depends heavily on your revenue level and how much of Kajabi's toolset you actually use.

Can I migrate my existing courses from one platform to another?

Yes, but the ease of migration varies significantly by platform. Most platforms allow you to export student data as a CSV and manually recreate your course structure in the new system. Some platforms offer dedicated migration support or one-click import tools, particularly if you're coming from a competitor. The bigger challenge is typically migrating your email list and active memberships without disrupting access for paying students. It's best practice to run both platforms in parallel for 30–60 days during a migration, giving students time to transition smoothly. Always back up all course content, student records, and purchase history before beginning any platform switch. If you have thousands of active students, consider hiring a specialist to handle the technical migration rather than doing it manually. Results may vary depending on the volume of content and student data involved.

Which platform is best if I want to build both a course and a paid community?

If you want both a structured course experience and a thriving paid community under one roof, your best options in 2026 are Kajabi or Alternative B — but for different reasons. Kajabi has made significant improvements to its community features and works well if you want a single billing relationship with your students covering both course access and community membership. Alternative B, on the other hand, delivers a more immersive, engagement-focused community experience with better discussion tools, live events, and member networking — though its course delivery is more limited. The right choice depends on whether the course or the community is your primary product. If the course is the hero and community is a bonus, Kajabi. If the community is the core transformation and courses are supplemental content, Alternative B. As always, individual results will vary based on how actively you moderate and engage within your community.
