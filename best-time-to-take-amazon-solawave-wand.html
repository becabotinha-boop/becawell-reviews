Best Time to Use the Solawave Wand (And How to Get the Most Out of Every Session)

If you just unboxed your Solawave Wand and you're wondering when to work it into your routine, you're not alone. Timing your skincare tools can make a real difference — and with a device that combines red light therapy, microcurrent, therapeutic warmth, and vibration massage, getting the when right is just as important as the how.

The short answer: nighttime, after cleansing, before your serums. But there's a bit more nuance worth knowing if you want to squeeze every bit of value out of this tool.

Why Nighttime Is the Sweet Spot

Your skin does its heaviest repair work while you sleep. Cell turnover accelerates, collagen production ramps up, and your barrier works to recover from the day's environmental stress. Using the Solawave Wand in the evening means you're giving your skin a head start — the red light therapy and microcurrent stimulate circulation and support that natural repair cycle right before it kicks into high gear.

There's also a practical reason: red light therapy can make your skin temporarily more sensitive to UV exposure. Using it at night eliminates any concern about stepping outside right after your session. Some users report a warm, flushed glow immediately after use — that's a completely normal response to increased circulation, and it tends to settle within a few minutes.

That said, morning use isn't off the table. Some people prefer to use it first thing to depuff around the eyes and jaw. If mornings work better for your schedule, that's fine — just make sure you apply SPF before going outside afterward, no exceptions.

Where in Your Routine Does It Go?

This is where people get tripped up. The Solawave Wand works best with a conductive serum — the brand sells its own Activating Serum specifically for this purpose, but you can also use a water-based hyaluronic acid serum as a substitute. The serum helps the microcurrent and warmth penetrate more effectively and prevents any dragging on the skin.

Here's the step-by-step order that works well for most people:

  1. Cleanse thoroughly — remove makeup, SPF, and any oil. You want a clean surface.
  2. Apply a thin layer of serum — just enough for the wand to glide smoothly.
  3. Use the Solawave Wand — 3 to 5 minutes across the face, moving in upward, outward motions.
  4. Apply your remaining serums and moisturizer — your skin is primed for absorption at this point.
  5. If morning: SPF last.

Avoid using it over active acne, broken skin, or irritated patches. The warmth and stimulation are great for fine lines and dullness, but they can aggravate inflamed skin.

How Often Should You Use It?

Most dermatologists and the brand itself suggest starting at 5 times per week and adjusting based on how your skin responds. Individual results vary significantly depending on skin type, age, and existing skincare routine. Some users report visible improvements in skin texture and firmness within 3 to 4 weeks of consistent use. Others notice changes more gradually over 6 to 8 weeks.

Results may vary, and there's no universal timeline. If your skin feels overstimulated or tight after daily sessions, pulling back to every other day is a completely reasonable adjustment.

FAQ

Can I use the Solawave Wand every day, or is it possible to overdo it?

Using the Solawave Wand daily is generally considered safe for most skin types, and the brand does recommend up to 5 sessions per week for best results. That said, "more is more" doesn't always apply to skincare technology. If you notice increased dryness, sensitivity, or any irritation, your skin is telling you to ease up. Some users report that alternating days — rather than daily use — actually gives their skin better recovery time between sessions, which can improve results over the long run. A good rule of thumb: start at 3 to 4 times per week for the first two weeks, monitor how your skin responds, and scale up from there. Individual results vary, so tune into your own skin rather than following someone else's schedule exactly.

Does it matter which serum I use with the Solawave Wand, or can I use any product I have?

The serum matters more than most people realize. The Solawave Wand's microcurrent and warmth need a conductive medium to work effectively — that's why a water-based serum is essential. Oil-based products don't conduct as well and can also cause the wand to drag uncomfortably across the skin. The brand's own Activating Serum is formulated specifically for use with the device and contains ingredients meant to support the treatment. That said, a plain hyaluronic acid serum works as a solid substitute if you're not ready to add another product purchase. Avoid using thick creams or oils as your base layer. And never use the wand on completely dry skin — you risk irritation and you won't get the same level of efficacy. Some users report that pairing it with a vitamin C serum in the morning gives a nice brightening boost, though nighttime use with a hydrating serum tends to be the most popular approach overall.
