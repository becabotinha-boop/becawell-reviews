Frequently Asked Questions

Will it fit on my current desk without hanging over the edge?

Most standing desk converters in this category are designed for desks that are at least 24 inches deep and 48 inches wide. The converter itself typically has a footprint of about 35–47 inches wide and 22–24 inches deep. Measure your desk surface before ordering — you want at least 2–3 inches of clearance around the converter's base so it sits stable and leaves room for papers or a lamp beside it.

How much weight can it hold — will it handle two monitors?

The weight capacity is typically rated around 33 lbs for the full platform. Two 24-inch monitors will run about 18–22 lbs combined. Add a laptop and keyboard and you're comfortably under the limit. If you're running two large 27-inch or 32-inch monitors, verify the combined weight — some heavier screens can push close to the limit and you may notice more sway at full standing height with a max-weight load.

Is it actually stable at standing height or does it wobble when I type?

At mid-range heights (10–15 inches of lift), the X-frame design is stable enough that light-to-moderate typing doesn't produce distracting vibration. At the maximum lift height with heavier monitors, there is minor sway if you type hard or rest your wrists on the edge. Most users describe it as acceptable rather than bothersome. If you're a heavy typist or use a mechanical keyboard with significant keystroke force, the wobble at max height may be more noticeable.

Do I need a separate monitor arm, or does it come with one?

The converter's upper platform holds your monitor directly — no arm included or required. Your monitor just sits on the elevated surface. If you want to tilt or pivot your monitor independently of the platform height, you can add a monitor arm that clamps to the upper tier, but it's not necessary for standard use. The platform itself has enough depth to angle a monitor slightly by hand if needed.

How long does assembly take, and do I need tools?

Most users report assembly in 15–25 minutes. The converter ships in two main pieces — the frame and the surface tiers — that click or bolt together with minimal hardware. A screwdriver is typically needed for a few bolts securing the keyboard tray; everything else is tool-free. The instructions are straightforward. Initial height calibration for your specific sitting and standing heights takes another 5 minutes once assembled.
