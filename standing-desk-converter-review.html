Frequently Asked Questions

Will this fit on a standard 60-inch desk without taking over the whole surface?

Yes, comfortably. The base footprint when collapsed is roughly 23 by 22 inches, and most 60-inch desks leave meaningful space on either side. In standing position the platform rises vertically, so it doesn't expand outward. You'll still have room for a notebook, water bottle, and small items alongside it. If your desk is narrower than 48 inches, measure first — it'll fit but feel tight.

Can it hold two monitors at once?

Two 24-inch monitors can fit side by side on the upper platform, but it's snug — you're at roughly the weight limit and the platform edges. Most users going dual-monitor report it works but recommend keeping total monitor weight under 25 lbs combined. A 27-inch plus a smaller secondary in portrait mode is a more comfortable configuration. If your primary setup is two 27-inch or larger monitors, a wider converter or a motorized desk is a better fit.

How much assembly is required out of the box?

Most users report 15–25 minutes of assembly. The frame connects in a few bolt points with the included Allen wrench, the keyboard tray attaches with two screws, and the monitor platform locks into position. No special tools needed beyond what's in the box. The instructions are adequate but not detailed — a few YouTube unboxing videos for similar X-frame converters can help if you get stuck on the frame alignment step.

Does it wobble when typing or when the platform is fully extended?

At mid-range heights (the sweet spot for most standing use), there's minimal wobble during normal typing. At full extension — near the top of the 19-inch range — there's a small amount of sway noticeable if you type hard or rest your wrists on the platform. Most users work in the middle two-thirds of the height range and don't encounter this. If you're tall and need maximum height regularly, this is worth knowing before you buy.

Is the keyboard tray large enough for a full-size keyboard and mouse?

The keyboard tray measures approximately 23 inches wide and 9.5 inches deep. A standard full-size keyboard fits cleanly. A tenkeyless or compact keyboard leaves room for a mouse on the tray. A full-size keyboard with a large mouse pad side-by-side is tight — most users in that situation keep the mouse on the main platform or on a separate surface. If you use a large gaming mouse pad, factor this into your decision.
