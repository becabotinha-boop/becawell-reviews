I tested this with winter sweaters, and the volume reduction was about 35–40%. Not magical, but significant enough that you'll notice.

Will the zippers break after a few trips?

No—these have heavy-duty nylon zippers, not plastic. After 40 trips, mine still glide smoothly without jamming. The reinforced seams prevent the zipper from catching fabric. Generic cubes often fail here by trip 8–10. These are built differently.

What size suitcase do I pair these with?

The six-piece set fits best in a 24–28 inch carry-on or medium checked bag (think 60–75 liters). If you're using a small carry-on (40–50 liters), the two XL cubes might not fit flat. For a small rolling suitcase, a 3-piece set would be better. Measure your suitcase interior before ordering.

Do clothes wrinkle inside the compressed cubes?

Compression minimizes wrinkles for synthetic blends and knits. Natural fibers like linen and cotton wrinkle more. The key is folding clothes neatly before placing them in the cube. If you fold well, minimal wrinkles. If you stuff them chaotically, compression makes it worse. It's not a wrinkle-prevention tool; it's a volume-reduction tool.

Can I use these for things other than clothes?

Absolutely. I use them for toiletries, electronics, shoes, and even light snacks. The mesh panels let you see what's inside, so you're not guessing. The compression is weaker on shoes or hard items, but the organization benefit still applies. One reviewer uses them for seasonal storage in closets.
