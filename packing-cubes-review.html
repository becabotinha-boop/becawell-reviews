Frequently Asked Questions

Will all six cubes fit inside a standard carry-on like a 22-inch Away or Travelpro?

Yes — the set is designed around a standard 22x14x9 carry-on footprint. With compression zippers closed, all six cubes fit in that size bag with room for shoes along the side. The XL cube takes up roughly half the main compartment; the remaining five stack into the other half without forcing the zipper.

How do the cubes hold up after repeated washing? Do the zippers stay smooth?

After 14 uses and about 8 washes on gentle cycle, the zippers on my set are still smooth with no snagging. The DWR coating does diminish slightly after several washes — expected for any water-resistant fabric. Tumble dry low or air dry to preserve the coating longest. The ripstop nylon itself shows no pilling, fraying, or color fade after that many cycles.

Are these TSA-friendly? Do I need to take them out at security?

You don't need to remove the cubes from your bag. The mesh top panels are transparent enough that screeners can visually confirm clothing contents. Electronics should still go in a separate bin per TSA rules, but having cables in the small cube keeps them visually organized on the X-ray belt, which actually speeds up the screening process in my experience.

Can I use these cubes in a checked bag, or are they only for carry-on?

They work in checked bags too — the organization benefit is the same regardless of bag size. In a larger checked bag, you might find that three or four cubes do the job and leave the others for a separate bag or trip. Some travelers use the full six-piece set across a checked bag plus a personal item, splitting the cubes between both bags for an organized two-bag system.

What's the difference between the compression zipper and the main zipper?

The main zipper opens the cube fully so you can access everything inside. The compression zipper runs around the perimeter of the top panel and cinches the cube flat — it reduces the stuffed height by roughly 30–35%. You use the main zipper to pack, then run the compression zipper to flatten the cube before loading it into your bag. Both zippers are independent and both stay functional long-term.
