Frequently Asked Questions

Can the 5.8QT basket really fit enough food for a family dinner?

For 2–3 people, yes, comfortably. For 4 people eating a full protein-and-vegetable meal, you'll likely need two back-to-back rounds — one for the protein, one for the vegetables. Each round takes 15–25 minutes, so the total time is still faster than oven roasting everything together. For 5 or more people eating simultaneously, a larger countertop oven is a better tool.

Is the basket actually dishwasher safe, or does that ruin the non-stick coating?

The basket and tray are rated dishwasher safe by the manufacturer. Most owners run them on the top rack and report no coating issues after months of regular use. To be safe with the non-stick surface long-term, hand washing with warm soapy water takes under a minute and avoids any wear from harsh dishwasher detergents — but both options are genuinely viable, which is more than most non-stick cookware offers.

How loud is it during operation? Can I have a conversation or watch TV nearby?

It runs at a moderate fan noise — noticeable in a quiet kitchen but not disruptive. It's roughly comparable to a bathroom exhaust fan in volume. You can hold a conversation, watch TV in the adjacent room, or have a video call in a different part of the house without the noise being intrusive. It's significantly quieter than most blenders or food processors.

Does it actually use less oil than pan-frying, and does the food taste different?

Yes, meaningfully less. Most air fryer recipes use a light coating of spray oil or a teaspoon of oil tossed on the food, versus tablespoons needed to pan-fry. The result is different from deep-fried food — less rich, a bit lighter — but the texture is genuinely crispy on the outside and cooked through on the inside. Vegetables like broccoli and Brussels sprouts come out with caramelized edges that most people find better than pan-fried versions.

What happens if I cook something that smokes a lot — like bacon or fatty meat?

Fatty proteins like bacon can produce smoke in an air fryer if the drippings accumulate and overheat. The standard fix is to add a small amount of water (about 2 tablespoons) to the bottom tray under the basket, which prevents drippings from burning. Cooking fatty meats at a slightly lower temp (340–360°F instead of 400°F) also reduces smoke. Most users cook bacon successfully with this approach — it just requires that small adjustment rather than running it at max heat.
