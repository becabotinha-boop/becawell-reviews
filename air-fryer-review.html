Frequently Asked Questions

Do I really need the 5.8-quart size, or would a smaller air fryer work?

Smaller models (3–4 quarts) force you to batch cook, doubling your time investment. The 5.8-quart fits a whole protein and a full vegetable portion in one cycle. For weeknight cooking or meal prep, the bigger basket cuts your total time in half. If you're cooking for one or two people occasionally, smaller works. For families or serious meal prep, the extra basket volume justifies the counter space.

Does frozen food actually cook without thawing?

Yes. Frozen chicken breasts cook evenly in 18–20 minutes; the rapid air circulation thaws and cooks simultaneously. Frozen vegetables cook even faster (14–16 minutes). The only catch: you might need to add 2–3 minutes to what the recipe says. Start checking at the lower time, not the upper. Fish and thinner cuts thaw fastest; dense items like whole potatoes need planning ahead.

How loud is it, and does it smell?

It's quieter than a convection oven or toaster oven—a gentle hum, not a roar. You can have a conversation next to it. The smell is just food cooking: if you're roasting chicken, you get roasted chicken smell, not chemical fumes. Some lingering smoke odor if you're cooking at high temp, but that's any cooking method, not specific to this model.

Is the nonstick coating safe, and how long does it actually last?

The coating is PTFE-based (similar to traditional nonstick pans), which is safe at normal cooking temps under 500°F. This model maxes at 400°F, so it's in the safe zone. After six months of regular use, the coating showed no peeling in testing. It will eventually wear, but you're looking at a year or two of heavy use before replacement becomes necessary, not weeks like some budget models.

Will this replace my oven, or do I still need one?

It won't fully replace an oven. This excels at roasting and crisping; it's not ideal for baking bread or cakes, or for cooking large casseroles. Think of it as a second appliance that handles 70% of your cooking. You'll still use the oven occasionally, but not for weeknight rotisserie-style proteins and vegetables, which is where real time saves happen.
