Frequently Asked Questions

Do blue light blocking glasses actually reduce eye strain, or is it placebo?

The research is mixed at the clinical level, but the practical evidence from consistent users is fairly clear: eye fatigue during and after extended screen sessions decreases for most people. The mechanism isn't just blue light filtering — the anti-reflective coating also reduces ambient glare that contributes to strain. Whether that's a direct biological effect or a reduction in squinting-related muscle fatigue, the outcome is the same for most users. It's worth a one-week trial at this price point to find out for yourself.

Will the amber tint bother me, and does it affect color accuracy on my monitor?

The tint is noticeable the first day and most people stop perceiving it by day three or four as their brain adjusts. It does shift color temperature slightly toward warm — your screen will look similar to Night Mode at a low setting. For general work, email, writing, and coding, this is a non-issue. For color-critical work like photo editing or video grading, it creates a problem because you can't accurately assess color. If that's your primary use case, look for clear-lens blue light glasses, which filter less blue light but don't distort color perception.

Can I wear these over my prescription glasses?

Not comfortably with most prescription frames — the fit isn't designed to overlay another pair of glasses, and the pressure on your nose and ears becomes uncomfortable quickly. If you wear contact lenses, these work perfectly as a standalone pair. If you rely on prescription glasses and want blue light protection, the right solution is prescription blue light lenses from an optician, which run $80–150 depending on your prescription complexity and the lens upgrade you choose.

How much blue light do these actually block, and does the percentage matter?

These filter 90%+ of light in the 400–450nm range, which is the high-energy portion most associated with eye strain and melatonin suppression. The percentage matters more for evening use than daytime use — if your goal is avoiding sleep disruption, you want a higher filter rate in the 90%+ range. For daytime eye strain reduction alone, even 50–60% filtering shows measurable benefit. The amber tint is the visual indicator of filter strength — the warmer the lens, the higher the percentage being blocked.

How long do these last before the coating starts degrading or the frame loosens?

With normal daily use — on and off once or twice a day, stored in a case — TR90 frames with spring hinges typically hold their shape and lens quality for 12–18 months before you start seeing wear. The coating can scratch if you wipe the lenses dry without using a microfiber cloth. The main failure mode on cheaper frames is hinge loosening after a few months of use; the spring-loaded hinges on this pair resist that significantly. Most users replace them annually as a maintenance purchase rather than because of a specific failure.
