Frequently Asked Questions

Will my feet hurt wearing these?

Initially, yes—your foot muscles are essentially waking up after years in supportive shoes. Soreness typically peaks around day 7-10, then fades by week 3-4. This isn't injury pain; it's adaptation. To minimize it, wear them 2-3 days per week for the first month while continuing to wear your regular shoes other days. Your body adapts faster with gradual exposure.

Are these actually good for trail running or just casual walking?

Both, but context matters. For light hiking and uneven terrain, they're excellent—the minimal sole and wide toe box actually improve stability on rocks and roots. For competitive trail running, maybe not; minimal shoes require significant gait adjustment and calf strength most runners don't have trained. Start with daily walking, then test on trails.

Do I need to use arch inserts or orthotics with these?

No—that defeats the purpose. The whole point is to let your arch build its own strength.
