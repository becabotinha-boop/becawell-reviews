Frequently Asked Questions

Will this work on thick or coarse hair?

It can work on thick hair, but with more limitations. You'll need to use the highest heat setting (410°F) and very small sections — closer to half an inch than one inch. Curl hold is shorter on coarse hair types, so a strong-hold mousse or setting spray applied before styling makes a real difference. For very thick or extra-dense hair, a larger-barrel professional curler may give you more consistent results.

Is it safe for color-treated or bleached hair?

Yes, with the right heat setting. Use 300°F–320°F for bleached or heavily processed hair — the low end of the range is specifically where you want to be. Avoid the top heat setting (410°F) on chemically treated hair. Applying a heat protectant spray before styling is still recommended regardless of tool, especially if your hair is already compromised from lightening treatments.

How long does it take to curl a full head of hair?

For shoulder-length, medium-density hair in one-inch sections: approximately 12–15 minutes once you're comfortable with the motion. Fine hair takes closer to 10 minutes. Thicker or longer hair — mid-back and below — runs 18–25 minutes depending on how many sections you work. The learning curve is real but short; most users report the second session takes noticeably less time than the first.

My hair gets tangled in automatic curlers. Will this happen with this one?

Tangling is almost always a sectioning issue rather than a device defect. The number one cause is feeding in too much hair at once. Stick strictly to one-inch sections, make sure hair is fully detangled and dry before you start, and feed the hair in smoothly rather than pushing it. If a tangle does occur, most units have a reverse direction button — press it immediately rather than pulling, which is what causes real damage.

How long do the curls actually last?

On fine to medium hair with no product: 4–6 hours before curls loosen to soft waves. With a light mousse applied to damp hair before styling and a quick mist of flexible-hold hairspray after: 8–10 hours is realistic. Humidity is the main variable — in high-humidity climates, a humidity-blocking spray at the end of styling extends hold significantly. Coarse or straight hair types will see shorter hold even at higher heat.
