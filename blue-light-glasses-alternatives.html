What's the Difference Between Prescription and Non-Prescription Blue Light Glasses?

Non-prescription blue light glasses are designed for people with normal vision who primarily need blue light filtering without vision correction. They feature clear lenses with blue light filtering coatings and work well for individuals without refractive errors like myopia or hyperopia. Prescription blue light glasses, conversely, incorporate your specific vision correction into the lenses while also providing blue light filtering—essentially combining two functionalities into one pair of eyewear. If you already wear prescription glasses or contacts, prescription blue light glasses eliminate the need for switching between multiple pairs of eyeglasses throughout the day. This is particularly beneficial for productivity and consistency of eye protection. The main advantage is convenience; instead of wearing regular prescription glasses followed by blue light glasses over top, you have one unified solution. However, prescription options are typically more expensive than non-prescription alternatives. Individual results vary regarding which option is best for your situation. Results may vary depending on whether your current prescription is current, how frequently you wear glasses, and your specific visual needs. If you wear contacts, non-prescription blue light glasses work perfectly over contacts. If you have significant refractive errors requiring constant correction, prescription blue light glasses are highly recommended for seamless eye protection and vision correction combined.
