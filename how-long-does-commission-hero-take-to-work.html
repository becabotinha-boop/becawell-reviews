How Long Does Commission Hero Take to Work?

If you're thinking about joining Commission Hero — Robby Blanchard's ClickBank affiliate training program — one of the first questions you're probably asking is: how long before I actually make money? It's a fair question, and you deserve a straight answer instead of hype.

The short answer: most students start seeing their first results between 4 and 12 weeks, but that range depends heavily on how fast you move through the training, how much you invest in ads, and how well you optimize your campaigns based on real data. Some users report seeing their first commissions within 2–3 weeks; others take 3–6 months before things click. Individual results vary significantly.

What "Working" Actually Looks Like With Commission Hero

Commission Hero teaches a specific paid traffic model: you run Facebook (and now other platform) ads that send people directly to a bridge page, which then directs them to a ClickBank offer. There's no SEO, no blogging, no long build-up. In theory, once your funnel is live and funded, the feedback loop is fast.

But "fast" is relative. Here's a realistic breakdown of what the timeline looks like for most people:

Week 1–2: Setup and Learning

You're watching modules, setting up your ClickBank account, picking an offer, and building your first bridge page. No money is being spent on ads yet — and no money is coming in. This phase feels slow, but rushing it is the #1 mistake new students make. If your funnel is built wrong, no amount of ad spend will save you.

Week 3–4: First Ad Campaigns Go Live

This is where most students start spending real money — typically $10–$30/day to test. You're collecting data, not expecting profits yet. Some users report a first commission or two during this phase, which is encouraging but not yet a pattern. Think of this as paying for data, not paying for results.

Week 5–8: Optimization Phase

By now you have enough data to know what's working. You kill the losing ad sets, scale the winners, and start to see consistent clicks converting into commissions. This is where Commission Hero is designed to produce its first real "it's working" moment for students who follow the process.

Week 9–12+: Scaling or Pivoting

Students who hit a profitable funnel by this stage start scaling ad spend. Those who haven't usually need to revisit their offer selection, ad creative, or targeting. This is normal — results may vary and depend largely on how aggressively you test and iterate.

What Speeds Up (or Slows Down) Your Results

A few factors have an outsized impact on your timeline:

  • Ad budget: More daily spend means faster data collection. Students with $50+/day budgets typically reach profitability faster than those spending $5/day.
  • Offer selection: Some ClickBank offers convert far better than others. Choosing a proven, high-gravity offer early on removes a major variable.
  • How quickly you complete the training: Some students binge the course in a week; others take two months. Speed of implementation matters.
  • Willingness to lose money while learning: The first few weeks of ad spend are essentially a tuition fee. Students who get discouraged and quit during the loss phase never reach the profit phase.
  • Access to coaching: Commission Hero includes a private Facebook group and some coaching access. Students who actively engage with the community and ask questions tend to troubleshoot faster.

What Commission Hero Won't Tell You Upfront

The marketing for Commission Hero often highlights students earning $1,000+/day. Those results exist — but they're not typical, especially not in the first 30 days. The program requires real financial risk (ongoing ad spend), consistent effort, and tolerance for a learning curve that includes losing campaigns before finding winning ones.

Most independent reviews from actual students suggest a realistic first-90-days target of breaking even or generating modest profits, not replacing a full-time income. Some users report earning their first $500–$1,000 in commissions within 60 days; others are still testing at month 4. Individual results vary.

If your budget is very limited (under $300 total for ads), it's worth being honest with yourself: the program may work eventually, but the timeline stretches considerably when you can't run enough tests to find a winning campaign quickly.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I make money with Commission Hero if I've never run Facebook ads before?

Yes, but your timeline will likely be longer than someone with prior paid traffic experience. Commission Hero is designed to be beginner-friendly, and the training walks through ad setup step by step. That said, there's a genuine skill gap between watching a tutorial and running a profitable ad campaign. Expect to spend the first month learning the mechanics — pixel setup, audience targeting, ad creative testing — before you have the intuition to optimize effectively. Most beginners report that it took them 2–3 failed campaigns before the training concepts really clicked in a practical sense. That's completely normal and part of the process. The students who succeed aren't the ones who got it right on the first try — they're the ones who kept iterating based on data. Some users report accelerating this curve significantly by studying inside the Commission Hero Facebook group and modeling ad angles that already work for other students in the same niche.

Is Commission Hero still worth it in 2025–2026, or has the market changed too much?

Paid affiliate traffic on social platforms is more competitive than it was when Commission Hero launched, but the core model — run targeted paid traffic to a high-converting ClickBank offer — still works. The main shift is that ad costs on Facebook have increased, which raises the bar on what "profitable" looks like and how much testing budget you need to reach it. Some users report pivoting to complementary platforms like TikTok Ads or YouTube Ads after learning the fundamentals in Commission Hero, which has extended the model's relevance. The training itself is periodically updated, though some students note that certain platform-specific modules (like Facebook ad interface walkthroughs) can go out of date between updates. If you're evaluating the program in 2026, check recent student results in the community — the methodology is sound, but your specific results may vary based on your niche, budget, and the competitive landscape at the time you launch.
