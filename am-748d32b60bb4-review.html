Frequently Asked Questions

Does it work with a phone case, or do I need to remove my case?

It works with most phone cases. The cradle has flexible side grips that accommodate cases up to about 4mm thick, which covers the majority of standard slim and medium-duty cases. Very bulky rugged cases (think OtterBox Defender) may fit loosely or not quite center correctly. If your case adds significant width, the 12-inch version gives you a little more clearance in the cradle.

How much does it actually magnify the image? Is it like 2x or more?

Real-world magnification is roughly 2x to 2.5x depending on your phone screen size and the viewing distance. A 6.1-inch screen feels roughly equivalent to watching a 13-inch screen from a normal seated distance. It's not a dramatic cinema-scale transformation, but it's meaningfully larger than holding your phone in your hand. The effect is most noticeable when reading text — small fonts become comfortable without zooming in.

Will it work with video streaming apps like Netflix or YouTube?

Yes, and this is actually where it shines most. Any app that displays content on your phone screen works — the magnifier is purely optical, with no electronics involved. Netflix, YouTube, FaceTime, TikTok, reading apps, navigation — all work. The one limitation is that the screen needs to stay on, so for long sessions make sure your phone's auto-lock is set to a longer interval or disabled, since you can't tap the screen easily while it's in the stand.

Is the image quality sharp, or does it look blurry or distorted?

Sharp in the center third of the image, slightly softer toward the edges. In practice, this matters less than it sounds — when you're watching a video, your eyes naturally focus on the center. For text reading, the center-to-midpoint area is crisp enough for comfortable reading. If you're extremely sensitive to image quality, this will bother you; for most people it becomes invisible within a few minutes of use.

How sturdy is the stand? I'd be using it on a kitchen counter near a sink.

The stand is stable on a flat, dry surface with no vibration. It's lightweight — that's what makes it portable — but the tradeoff is that it tips more easily than a weighted base would. On a kitchen counter that's not near active movement, it holds fine. I wouldn't set it directly next to a sink where it could get splashed, and I'd keep it a few inches from the edge since it doesn't have rubber feet gripping the surface. A small plate behind it is a practical stabilizer if your counter has any slope.
