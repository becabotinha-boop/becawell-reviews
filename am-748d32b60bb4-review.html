Can I use this for video calls, or is there a delay or distortion issue?

You can use it for video calls. Since the magnifier is purely optical — no electronics involved — there's no processing delay. The person on screen appears larger and clearer. Is the image quality actually HD or is that just marketing language?

The lens itself doesn't add resolution — your phone's screen quality determines the base image. What 'HD' refers to here is that the Fresnel lens design doesn't degrade the image the way flat acrylic sheets do. Older flat magnifiers introduce noticeable blurring and color fringing at the edges. This design maintains the sharpness of your phone's original display across most of the viewing area, which is a meaningful real-world difference.

How sturdy is the stand? I'm worried it'll tip over easily.

The stand is stable on a flat surface under normal conditions — it holds a phone without tipping during regular use. It's not heavy-duty; if knocked it will fall. Think of it as comparable to a lightweight plastic picture frame stand, not a weighted monitor base. For a desk or nightstand it's perfectly adequate. If you need something more robust for a busier environment, it may disappoint — but for its intended use case, most buyers find it functional.

Does it work in landscape mode for watching movies?

It works in landscape, but the experience is more natural in portrait orientation. The lens and stand are optimized for vertical phone positioning. In landscape mode, you may need to adjust the stand angle more carefully, and some users find the image proportions slightly off. For movies and widescreen content specifically, you'll get better results if you try both orientations and settle on what looks clearest for your specific phone and content type.
