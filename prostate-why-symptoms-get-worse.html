The Mechanism

As men age, testosterone converts to DHT (dihydrotestosterone) at an accelerating rate. DHT binds to prostate cells and triggers growth — causing the prostate to enlarge and press against the urethra. The result: weak flow, frequent urination, incomplete emptying. Addressing the testosterone-to-DHT conversion pathway is the key mechanism that makes natural supplements work.

What the Research Shows

  • After 40, DHT levels rise 20-30% as conversion accelerates
  • Enlarged prostate physically compresses the urethra — causing weak flow
  • Beta-sitosterol and saw palmetto block 5-alpha-reductase (the DHT-conversion enzyme)
  • Zinc deficiency accelerates DHT buildup — most men over 50 are deficient

Common Questions

Is an enlarged prostate dangerous?

Benign prostatic hyperplasia (BPH) is not cancer, but it significantly impacts quality of life. Untreated, it can lead to bladder complications. See a urologist for diagnosis before starting supplements.

Can supplements help without prescription medication?

For mild-to-moderate BPH symptoms, natural 5-alpha-reductase inhibitors have clinical support. For severe cases, prescription treatment may be necessary.

How long does it take?

60-90 days is the typical protocol. Some men notice urgency reduction within 4-6 weeks.
