Frequently Asked Questions

How long before I notice any real difference in how I feel day-to-day?

Most people report the first noticeable shift around days 10–14, once the morning check-in becomes automatic and the system has enough data to start personalizing suggestions. The evening wind-down protocol tends to show results faster — often within the first week — because sleep quality is highly responsive to consistent pre-sleep cues. Don't expect dramatic changes in the first three days; the value is cumulative.

Is this something I have to use every single day, or does it work intermittently?

The adaptive feedback loop requires consistent daily input to function well — it needs roughly 14 days of data before it can identify patterns meaningful enough to act on. That said, missing a day here and there doesn't reset your progress. What matters is averaging five or more days per week in the first month. After that, most users find the habit so embedded that gaps become rare rather than the rule.

I already use a fitness tracker and a sleep app. Will this overlap with what I already have?

There's some overlap in the data-collection layer, but the protocols themselves are different from what fitness trackers and sleep apps provide. Those tools measure and report; Small Business uses your inputs to guide active interventions like breathing sequences and structured reset routines. If anything, the two can complement each other — your existing sleep data can inform the check-ins you log here, giving the adaptive system richer context to work with.

Can I use this if I'm already working with a therapist or healthcare provider?

Yes, and many users do. The tool is explicitly not a clinical intervention — it's a daily structure support. Nothing in the protocols conflicts with therapy or standard medical care. That said, it's worth mentioning to your provider that you're using it, especially the stress regulation components, so they have a full picture of what you're doing. Most clinicians are supportive of evidence-informed self-management tools used alongside professional care.

What happens if I travel or my schedule gets disrupted — does the habit fall apart?

The check-in and morning protocol are phone-based and require no equipment, which makes travel disruption less of an issue than you'd expect. The evening protocol is the most vulnerable to schedule changes — it requires a ten-minute window and a quiet space, both of which travel can compress. Most users adapt by doing a shortened version on travel days rather than skipping entirely. The system accommodates gaps without penalizing you or resetting streaks in a punishing way.
