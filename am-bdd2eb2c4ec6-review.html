Frequently Asked Questions

Does the IMIPAW feeder work if my WiFi goes down while I'm away?

Any meals already scheduled will still dispense because the schedule is stored on the device itself, not streamed from the cloud. You'll lose the ability to push manual meals or check feeding history until WiFi is restored, but your cat won't miss a scheduled meal during a brief outage. The USB backup power also ensures a power blip doesn't reset the device.

What kibble size works best with this feeder?

Standard small-to-medium dry kibble — roughly the size of a pea or smaller — works reliably. Larger, irregularly shaped kibble or anything with a very hard shell can occasionally cause partial jams at the dispenser opening. If your cat eats a specialty large-breed kibble, test it manually a few times before relying on it fully for scheduled meals.

Can I use this for two cats?

The feeder itself doesn't distinguish between cats — it dispenses to whoever is in front of it. Many owners use it for two cats successfully, especially if both cats eat at the same pace and don't compete aggressively. If one cat dominates the bowl, you'd need to supervise meals or consider a second feeder on a slightly offset schedule to keep them separated.

How long does the hopper last before I need to refill it?

Capacity depends on your cat's meal size and frequency. For a typical adult cat eating two to three small meals per day, most owners find they refill once every five to seven days. The low-food alert in the app notifies you before it's completely empty, which gives you a day or so of buffer rather than discovering an empty hopper at dinner time.

Is the setup process complicated? I'm not particularly tech-savvy.

Setup is manageable but does require a few steps: download the app, create an account, connect the feeder to your 2.4GHz or 5GHz WiFi network, and run through the pairing process. It took me about ten minutes on first setup. The instructions are clear enough, and the app walks you through it. If you've ever set up a smart plug or thermostat, this is a similar level of complexity.
