What Is Alpilean?

Alpilean is a dietary supplement that targets what its makers call "low inner body temperature" — a metabolic state linked to slower fat burning. Unlike most supplements that use stimulants (caffeine, synephrine) to force thermogenesis, Alpilean uses a blend of alpine plant extracts designed to gently normalize internal body temperature over time.

It was developed in the US, manufactured in an FDA-registered facility, and has accumulated substantial customer reviews since its launch. It's become one of the better-known names in the "mechanism-based" weight loss supplement category.

The Alpine Ice Hack — Is The Science Real?

The core claim references a study (widely cited as coming from Stanford, 2023) that found a strong correlation between low internal body temperature and obesity. The idea: people who are overweight tend to have lower core body temperatures, which corresponds to a slower resting metabolic rate.

Is this real? The correlation between core body temperature and metabolic rate is well-established in physiology. Brown adipose tissue (BAT) — the type of fat that generates heat — is more active in leaner individuals. This is not a fringe claim.

What's less proven is the causal direction — does low temperature cause obesity, or does obesity cause lower temperature? The honest answer is: probably both, in a feedback loop. What Alpilean targets is using thermogenic plant compounds to interrupt this loop.

Important context: The "alpine ice hack" marketing framing (drinking cold water + the supplement) is largely a social media embellishment. The supplement's mechanism doesn't require cold water. The core ingredient science stands on its own.
