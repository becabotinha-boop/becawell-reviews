Frequently Asked Questions

How long before I notice any difference?

Realistically, most people report noticing a consistent baseline effect between weeks two and four of daily use. Supplements in this category work by maintaining levels in the body over time, not by producing an acute or immediate reaction. If you're expecting something dramatic in the first 48 hours, that's not how this works — or how most legitimate supplements work. Give it a full 30-day bottle before deciding.

Can I take this with other supplements or medications?

For most healthy adults taking common supplements like vitamin D or magnesium, there are no known interactions with the ingredients in this formulation. That said, if you're on prescription medications — particularly blood thinners, diabetes medications, or thyroid drugs — you should check with your doctor or pharmacist before adding any supplement to your routine. The ingredients are well-studied, but individual medical situations vary.

Is one capsule per day actually enough, or do I need more?

The one-capsule daily protocol is the studied and recommended dose — not a minimum. Taking more won't accelerate results and may simply waste product. The formulation is designed around what the research supports as an effective daily intake, not around selling more capsules. This is one of the markers of a well-formulated product: the dose matches the evidence rather than erring toward excess to seem more powerful.

Does it need to be taken with food?

It can be taken with or without food. Some people find fat-soluble nutrients absorb better with a meal containing some dietary fat, so if you're taking it at breakfast, including some eggs or nut butter is a reasonable habit. That said, there's no strict requirement, and taking it on an empty stomach first thing in the morning works fine for most people. Consistency of timing matters more than the specific conditions.
