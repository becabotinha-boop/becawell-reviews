I tracked nerve pain symptoms daily for 30 days while testing NerveFresh — burning intensity, tingling frequency, and nighttime disturbance. This is what the daily log actually showed. I'm not going to oversell this: it helped meaningfully in some areas and didn't move the needle much in others.

My Baseline (Why I Tested This)

I've dealt with peripheral neuropathy for 3 years — burning feet, pins and needles in the hands at night, occasional numbness. It's been worst at night, which affected sleep, which affected everything else. I'd tried gabapentin (didn't tolerate the side effects), B-complex vitamins (helped somewhat), and several other supplements with mixed results.

NerveFresh caught my attention because it combines Alpha Lipoic Acid (the most researched compound for neuropathic symptoms) with the B-vitamin complex at therapeutic doses, plus botanical calming agents. The combination made mechanistic sense, so I ran a 30-day test with a daily symptom journal.

Who This Is Actually For

Based on the research and my results, NerveFresh seems most likely to help with: neuropathy related to nutritional deficiency (especially B12), diabetic peripheral neuropathy, and oxidative stress-driven nerve damage. ALA has the strongest evidence specifically in these categories.

If your neuropathy is from nerve compression, physical injury, or chemotherapy, the evidence base is weaker and you'd need to have lower expectations. The 60-day guarantee means you can test it properly and return it if it doesn't help.
