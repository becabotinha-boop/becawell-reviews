Commission Hero Before and After Results: What Actually Happens?

If you've been researching Commission Hero, you've probably seen screenshots of big ClickBank paydays and people claiming they went from zero to thousands of dollars a month. But what do the real before and after results look like — and what should you actually expect? Let's cut through the noise.

The Direct Answer

Commission Hero is a paid traffic affiliate marketing course created by Robby Blanchard, a top ClickBank affiliate. It teaches you to run Facebook and Instagram ads that send people to ClickBank offer landing pages — and earn commissions when those people buy.

Some users report meaningful income gains after completing the course and running campaigns — particularly those who stick with testing and have budget to invest in ads. However, results may vary significantly, and many people see slow or no returns before they figure out what works. This is not a passive income button. It is a paid advertising skill you have to learn, test, and refine.

What "Before" Usually Looks Like

Most people who find Commission Hero are in a similar position: they want to earn money online but don't have a product, email list, or big audience. They may have tried dropshipping, blogging, or other affiliate programs with mixed results. Their daily income from online work is close to zero — or completely zero.

Before starting, most students have no experience buying Facebook ads, no ClickBank account set up, and no system for tracking conversions. They're starting completely fresh, which is exactly who Commission Hero was built for.

What "After" Can Look Like — Realistically

This is where individual results vary the most. Here's a realistic breakdown based on what the course covers and what the paid ads learning curve actually looks like:

  • Weeks 1–2: Setting up your ClickBank account, picking an offer, building a simple Facebook ad funnel. No revenue yet — this is setup and learning.
  • Weeks 3–4: First campaigns are live. Most beginners burn through some ad budget here testing audiences and creatives. Some users report small commissions during this phase; others see nothing.
  • Month 2–3: If you're iterating — cutting losing ad sets, scaling winning ones — some students see their first consistent commissions. Others are still in testing mode.
  • Month 3–6: Students who stay consistent and keep optimizing report income ranging from a few hundred to a few thousand dollars per month. A smaller group scales further with larger ad budgets.

The course-advertised results (five-figure months, $1,000/day screenshots) represent top performers — typically people with some prior marketing experience or larger starting budgets. They are not the average outcome. Results may vary based on your niche choice, ad spend, how much you test, and how quickly you learn from data.

What Actually Changes for People Who See Results

The students who report positive before-and-after transformations tend to share a few things in common. They treated it like a business skill to develop, not a system to plug in and profit from immediately. They reinvested early commissions back into ad spend. They watched the campaign data daily and made adjustments. And they didn't quit after the first week of losses.

What changes for them isn't just income — it's understanding. They develop a working knowledge of Facebook Ads Manager, conversion tracking, offer selection, and audience targeting. Those are real, transferable skills regardless of what they earn in the first month.

The Honest Caveats

Commission Hero requires an ad budget on top of the course fee. Most coaches suggest $10–$20/day minimum to test effectively — that's $300–$600/month in ad spend before you make anything back. If that budget would cause real financial stress, the timing may not be right.

Facebook ad policies also change frequently. Some offers that worked well in previous years have faced more restrictions. Staying compliant and adapting to platform changes is an ongoing part of running this type of business.

Finally, ClickBank commissions can be high (50–75% on digital products), but so can refund rates on certain offers. Picking the right offer is half the battle, and Commission Hero does cover offer selection — but execution still matters.

Who This Works Best For

Commission Hero tends to produce the best results for people who are comfortable with data, have patience for a testing phase, and can commit to consistent effort over 3–6 months. It is not ideal for people looking for guaranteed income fast or those with very limited ad budgets.

Individual results vary based on your starting budget, how many hours you put into optimization, and how quickly you absorb the platform mechanics. Rushing the process or pausing campaigns too soon is one of the most common reasons people don't see the results they hoped for.

Is Commission Hero worth it if I have no marketing experience?

It can be, but it depends heavily on your expectations going in. Commission Hero is specifically designed for beginners — it walks through account setup, offer selection, ad creation, and scaling from scratch. Paid advertising on Facebook involves real money going out the door while you figure out what works, and that process takes time even with good training. Some users with zero prior experience do report building profitable campaigns after following the course closely. Others find the ad costs during the learning phase more than they expected. If you go in treating it as a skill-building investment rather than a shortcut, you'll likely get more out of it. Results may vary.
