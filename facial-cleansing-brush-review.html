Frequently Asked Questions

Is the Sonic Facial Cleansing Brush safe for sensitive skin?

Yes, with the right approach. Use it on the lowest speed setting and limit sessions to once daily rather than twice. Silicone bristles are gentler than nylon or foam, so the mechanical stimulation is mild. If you have rosacea or an actively compromised skin barrier, check with your dermatologist before adding any powered cleansing tool to your routine — this one included.

How often should I actually use it?

Most dermatologists recommend sonic cleansing brushes once daily — typically in the evening after removing makeup. Using it twice a day is fine for most normal-to-oily skin types, but if you notice tightness or increased sensitivity, pull back to evenings only. The brush does more cleansing work than hands alone, so more isn't always better. Sixty seconds once a day consistently will outperform five minutes occasionally.

Can I use it with my existing cleanser, or do I need a specific formula?

You can use it with whatever cleanser you're already using — gel, cream, foam, all work. The one format to avoid is physical scrubs with particles; you don't need them with a sonic brush, and the combination is more exfoliation than your skin needs. A gentle, non-abrasive cleanser is ideal. The brush does the mechanical work; the cleanser just needs to break down oil and product.

How do I clean the brush itself, and how long does it last?

Rinse the silicone head under warm water after every use — no special cleaner required. Because silicone is non-porous, bacteria don't accumulate the way they do with foam brush heads. A deeper clean with a drop of your face wash once a week keeps it fresh. Silicone doesn't degrade the way bristles do, so the brush head should last 12+ months of daily use without any quality change.

Will it help with blackheads and congested pores?

It helps more than manual cleansing, but it's not a blackhead extractor. What it does: consistently loosens the oxidized oil that causes blackheads to form, so over four to eight weeks of daily use, many users see existing congestion reduce and new blackheads form less frequently. If you have deep, established blackheads, you'll still need a targeted treatment — BHA exfoliant, for example — but the brush makes that treatment work better by keeping pores cleaner between uses.
