Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use this brush with my regular face wash, or do I need a special cleanser?

You can use it with any cleanser you already own — gel, foam, micellar, or cream. The brush amplifies whatever cleanser you use by increasing lather and absorption into pores. The one exception: avoid using it with physical scrubs or exfoliating cleansers that contain grit, since the combination of mechanical beads and sonic vibration is more than most skin needs at once and can cause microtears.

How often should I use it, and is daily use too much?

For normal to oily skin, daily use — once a day, typically at night to remove the day's buildup — is appropriate and what most users do. For dry or sensitive skin, three to four times per week is a safer starting point. The key signal is your skin's response: slight smoothness and improved texture means it's working well. Tightness, flaking, or increased sensitivity means you're overdoing it and should reduce frequency.

Will this help with blackheads on my nose?

It can reduce the appearance of blackheads over time, but it's not an extraction tool and won't produce overnight results. What it does is prevent the dead-skin buildup that makes pores look more congested and darker. Consistent daily use over four to six weeks — combined with a cleanser that contains salicylic acid or niacinamide — is where most users see meaningful improvement. Don't expect one use to make a visible difference.

How do you clean and maintain the brush head?

Rinse the silicone head under warm water after every use and let it air dry. Because silicone is non-porous, bacteria don't embed the way they do in nylon bristles, so there's no deep-cleaning ritual required. Once a week, a quick wipe with a gentle soap or a dab of micellar water keeps it fresh. The handle can be wiped down with a damp cloth. There are no replacement heads to buy or track — the head is designed to last the life of the device.

I have combination skin with a sensitive T-zone. Is this too harsh?

Combination skin is arguably the ideal use case for this brush. You can use the lower speed setting on dry or sensitive cheek areas and the higher setting on an oily T-zone — same device, adjusted pressure and speed. The key is using light, circular strokes without pressing hard. The vibration does the work; you don't need to add manual scrubbing pressure on top of it. Most combination-skin users find they can tolerate daily use without any reaction.
