Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use this brush if I have sensitive skin?

Many women with sensitive skin use it successfully on the low speed setting every other day rather than daily. The silicone head is significantly gentler than bristle brushes. That said, if your skin is currently inflamed, broken out in cysts, or dealing with a rosacea flare, hold off until your skin has calmed. Introduce it slowly — start with 30 seconds twice a week — and increase frequency only if your skin responds well.

Do I need to buy replacement heads, and how often?

Silicone heads don't degrade or splay the way bristle heads do, which is one of the practical advantages of this style of brush. Most manufacturers recommend replacing the head every 6–12 months, but many users find the silicone stays in good condition longer than that. Rinse it thoroughly after every use and let it air dry — that's genuinely enough maintenance for regular hygiene between replacements.

Will this brush help with clogged pores around my nose?

This is the use case where most reviewers report the most noticeable improvement. The vibration combined with a cleanser loosens the oxidized sebum that causes blackheads and visible pores. You won't see results in a day or two, but consistent daily use for two to four weeks typically makes a real difference. Pair it with a gentle salicylic acid cleanser for the nose zone for the best outcome.

Can I use it with any cleanser, or do I need a special formula?

Any cleanser you already use works fine — there's no proprietary formula required. Gel or foam cleansers tend to distribute most evenly with the vibrating head. Avoid anything with physical exfoliating beads or granules when using the brush, since the combination can be too aggressive for daily use. On nights you're using an exfoliating acid, skip the brush and let the acid do its work without additional mechanical stimulation.

How does this compare to a Clarisonic or professional-grade cleansing device?

Clarisonic discontinued its line in 2020, so comparisons are largely academic at this point. Professional-grade sonic brushes from brands like Foreo Luna run $100–200 and offer additional features like app connectivity and targeted zones for different skin areas. The core cleansing result — sonic vibration with a soft head — is mechanically similar. For most women who want cleaner skin without a premium price, the difference in outcome is marginal enough that the price gap isn't justified.
