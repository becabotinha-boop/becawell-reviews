Frequently Asked Questions

Will this work if I already have really sensitive skin?

It depends on what makes your skin sensitive. If you react to friction (scrubbing aggravates you), this is actually safer than manual cleansing because vibration doesn't require pressure. If you have reactive skin conditions like severe rosacea or active eczema, start with the lowest intensity and shortest duration (even 30 seconds), then slowly build. Many sensitive-skin reviewers report it's gentler than their previous cleansing methods. Patch-test on one cheek first if you're nervous.

How long until I see actual results?

Texture improvements (smoother, less bumpy) typically show within 7–10 days. Clearer skin and reduced congestion take 3–4 weeks. Dramatic changes (like significantly smaller pores or even skin tone) take 6–8 weeks. You need consistency: skipping days resets your progress. This isn't an overnight fix, but it's a real, visible fix if you're patient.

Can I use this with retinoids or vitamin C serum?

Yes, but be strategic. If you're using active ingredients daily, you might need to reduce cleansing frequency to once daily instead of twice. Active ingredients can make skin more sensitive to vibration. Start with once daily, observe your skin for one week, then adjust. Many users find this brush actually enhances their retinoid routine because clean skin absorbs it better, but you have to build tolerance carefully.

What kind of cleanser should I use with it?

Any gentle cleanser works. You don't need to buy a special branded cleanser—that's marketing. A fragrance-free gel, cream, or milk cleanser from any brand is fine. Some users prefer oil cleansers first (to dissolve makeup), then use the brush with a gentle water-based cleanser. The brush does the work; the cleanser just needs to be mild enough not to irritate. Avoid harsh scrubs or cleansers with strong fragrance—they work against the brush's gentleness.

Is this better than manual exfoliating or a Clarisonic knockoff?

Manual exfoliating (scrubs, physical exfoliants) relies on friction and pressure, which irritates sensitive skin and can trigger breakouts. Vibration-based cleansing (like this) is gentler and more effective for most people. Cheaper knockoffs exist, but reviewers consistently report they either vibrate too aggressively or stop working within weeks. This brand holds up to regular use and maintains consistent performance. You get what you pay for.
