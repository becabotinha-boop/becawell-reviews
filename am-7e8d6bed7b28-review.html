Frequently Asked Questions

How much floor space does the full rack actually take up?

The A-frame rack for a full 290–840 lb set typically runs around 68–72 inches wide and 24–30 inches deep depending on the configuration. Measure your space with those dimensions before ordering. You also want clearance on both sides to pull weights off the ends without bumping a wall. A 7-foot-wide clear zone is a reasonable minimum to work comfortably.

Are the rubber heads truly odor-free, or do they have that strong rubber smell?

New rubber dumbbells almost always have some off-gassing smell when first unpacked — the HANDBODE set is no exception. Most buyers report the smell dissipates within one to two weeks, especially with decent ventilation in the room. If you're setting this up in a bedroom with no airflow, leave a window open for the first few days. By week two it's typically a non-issue.

Will the hexagonal heads actually protect my floor, or do I still need mats?

The rubber encasement handles incidental contact and controlled set-downs on most hard floors without damage. For deliberate drops or if you're working on hardwood that you really care about, a rubber gym mat underneath is still smart practice. The hex heads prevent rolling and reduce impact force, but they're not a substitute for a mat if your workout style involves any dropping of weights.

Is this set worth it if I already own a few pairs of dumbbells?

Depends on what you own. If you have two or three pairs that cluster in a narrow range — say 20, 25, and 30 lbs — then yes, the full set fills in the gaps that are limiting your programming. If you already have a wide spread of weights from 15 to 60 lbs, buying this full set is redundant. In that case, filling specific missing increments individually would cost less and take up less room.

How difficult is the rack to assemble, and does it arrive with everything needed?

Assembly is straightforward for most buyers — the A-frame rack uses standard bolt connections and typically takes 30–45 minutes with the included hardware.
