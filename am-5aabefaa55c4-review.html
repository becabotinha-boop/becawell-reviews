It includes both. Concepts are well-developed enough to apply without buying anything specific — the zone-based organization chapter is a good example. How current is the 2026 trend content — will it feel dated quickly?

The 2026 trends covered — warm neutrals, intentional mixed metals, selective open display, textured surfaces — are positioned as multi-year directions rather than seasonal micro-trends. That's a smart editorial choice; the guide explicitly frames these as the aftermath of overcorrecting from the cold-gray minimalism of the 2015–2022 period. Most of the trend content should remain relevant for three to five years before feeling noticeably dated.

Can I use this if I'm renting and can't make structural changes?

Yes, and the guide is more renter-friendly than most design books. The refresh framework is built around non-permanent changes: hardware swaps (often renter-permissible with originals saved), organizational inserts, lighting changes via plug-in fixtures, and styling choices. The section on 'maximum impact, minimum commitment' changes is particularly useful for renters who want a significantly different-feeling kitchen without lease-violating modifications.
