Best Time to Start Commission Hero: An Honest, No-Hype Guide

If you've been circling Commission Hero for a while — watching the sales page, reading reviews, wondering whether now is actually a good moment to jump in — this article is for you. The short answer: the best time to start Commission Hero is when you can commit at least 10–15 focused hours per week and have a small ad budget ready to go. But there's more nuance to it than that, and getting the timing wrong is one of the most common reasons people don't see results from the program.

Let's break it down properly.

What Commission Hero Actually Is (Quick Context)

Commission Hero is an online training program created by Robby Blanchard — currently one of ClickBank's top affiliates — that teaches a paid Facebook ads approach to affiliate marketing. You pick a ClickBank offer, run Facebook ads, and earn commissions when people buy through your link.

This is not a slow-burn SEO or content blogging strategy. It's an active, paid-traffic model. That distinction matters a lot when we talk about timing.

The Direct Answer: Timing Comes Down to 3 Factors

1. Your Financial Runway

Commission Hero requires a Facebook ad budget. Robby is transparent about this — you'll need money to test ads before you find a winning campaign. Most students who report early traction mention spending anywhere from $200 to $1,000+ in the testing phase before seeing consistent returns. Individual results vary significantly, and some users report needing several months of testing before their campaigns become profitable.

The best time to start is when you have a dedicated ad budget set aside — money you can afford to use for testing without impacting your rent or bills. Starting when you're financially stretched tends to lead to cutting campaigns too early, which is one of the biggest momentum-killers in paid traffic.

2. Your Time Availability

This is a hands-on program. The training itself takes time to go through properly, and then you need consistent hours to set up ad accounts, create creatives, monitor campaigns, and optimize. Some users report managing this as a side hustle in evenings and weekends — but the learning curve is steeper when time is fragmented.

Ideally, start Commission Hero during a period when you have predictable, consistent availability. Summer months, a gap between jobs, or a season when your main job is quieter are all solid entry points. Starting during a notoriously busy stretch — holiday crunch, a major work project, exam season — usually means the training drags on and momentum is lost.

3. Your Mental Bandwidth for Learning Paid Ads

Facebook advertising has a learning curve. Ad account setups, pixel tracking, audience targeting, and ad creative testing are all skills you'll build through the program. Results may vary depending on how quickly you absorb the technical side and how quickly Facebook's algorithm optimizes your campaigns.

The best time is when you're not already overwhelmed. Starting Commission Hero when you're in a calm, focused headspace dramatically improves how much of the training actually sticks.

Is There a "Bad" Time to Start Commission Hero?

Yes — a few scenarios where the timing tends to work against you:

  • Q4 (October–December): Facebook ad costs spike during the holiday shopping season as e-commerce advertisers flood the platform. Your CPMs (cost per 1,000 impressions) can be 30–60% higher than in Q1. Some experienced affiliates navigate this well, but for beginners still learning the platform, launching during Q4 is an expensive classroom. Many Commission Hero users report better results starting campaigns in Q1 or Q2 when ad costs normalise.
  • When your ad account is brand new: Facebook's algorithm needs time and data to optimise. If you have zero ad history, expect a longer ramp-up period. This isn't a reason not to start — it's just a realistic expectation to have.
  • When you're undercapitalised: If you have less than $100–$200 set aside purely for ad spend, the testing phase will be very constrained. Results may vary widely, but undersized budgets tend to produce inconclusive data and frustration.

Realistic Expectations: What the Timeline Actually Looks Like

Commission Hero is not a "get rich in 30 days" scheme — and to his credit, Robby doesn't really sell it that way. Most students who report meaningful results describe a 2–4 month learning arc: the first month is setup and learning, the second is testing, and by month 3–4 some users report finding a profitable campaign combination. Others take longer. Individual results vary, and your niche selection, ad creative quality, and budget all play a role.

Some users report making their first commission within a few weeks. Others report months of testing before breaking even. Both outcomes are real. Going in with clear-eyed expectations about the learning curve — rather than expecting an instant income switch — is the single best thing you can do to set yourself up for success.

FAQ

Q: Does it matter which month of the year I start Commission Hero?

It matters more than most people think, but it's not the most important factor. From a pure ad cost perspective, January through March tends to be the most cost-efficient time to run Facebook ads, since the Q4 holiday surge has passed and competition drops. Some users report that launching test campaigns in Q1 gives them more data per dollar spent, which accelerates the learning curve. That said, you shouldn't wait indefinitely for the "perfect" month — starting in April, May, or June is still a solid window. The more important factor is your own financial and time readiness. A committed learner starting in October will typically outperform someone with perfect timing but low engagement. If you can, avoid starting new Facebook campaigns in November and December unless you already have a proven campaign you're scaling. Results may vary depending on niche competitiveness, creative quality, and offer selection regardless of the calendar month.

Q: Can I start Commission Hero while working a full-time job?

Many Commission Hero students do start while working full-time, and some users report successfully building their affiliate income as a side project before transitioning out of their day job. It's genuinely possible — but it requires honest self-assessment of your available time. If you can carve out a consistent 10–15 hours per week, including time to monitor active ad campaigns during business hours (since ad performance can shift quickly), it's workable. The challenge is that Facebook ad management benefits from reasonably timely attention — a campaign that's burning budget with poor results needs to be paused or adjusted, ideally within a day rather than a week. If your job demands are unpredictable or you're frequently unavailable for stretches of several days, that adds friction to the process. Individual results vary significantly between full-time students and part-time learners, but the program itself is structured in a way that accommodates both. The key is consistency over speed.
