Frequently Asked Questions

What are the exact dimensions of each size?

The two size variants are designed to cover narrow drawer and wide shelf use cases respectively — the smaller unit is proportioned for spaces in the 4–6 inch width range, the larger for 8–12 inch zones. Because listings can update, always check the current product page dimensions against your measured space before ordering. Measuring twice before buying an organizer is genuinely worth the 90 seconds it takes.

Will these actually stay put on a smooth pantry shelf?

On flat laminate or wood shelving, yes — the base lip provides enough contact friction that units don't migrate when you pull items out. On wire shelving the story is different: the lip geometry isn't designed to grip wire racks and units can shift or tip. If your shelves are wire, consider a shelf liner underneath or look for a product specifically designed for wire rack use.

Can I use these in the refrigerator?

The smooth walls and wipe-clean material make them practical for fridge use — condiment organization, deli drawer grouping, or snack zones work well. The material handles cold temperatures without warping. Keep in mind that condensation can cause items to slide more easily in a fridge environment than on a dry shelf, so taller stacks are better kept to two units high when refrigerated.

How do I keep the system looking organized after the first week?

The most honest answer: the system holds if you also reduce what you're storing. Organizers structure items — they don't create capacity that wasn't there. If a zone holds exactly as much as it reasonably should, the system stays intact with minimal effort. If it's overstocked, items will migrate back to disorder quickly regardless of container quality. A modest edit of what you're keeping does more for longevity than any bin will.

Do the two sizes stack with each other, or only same-size with same-size?

The stackable geometry is designed for same-size stacking — smaller on smaller, larger on larger. Cross-size stacking is physically possible in some configurations but the contact surface is reduced and stability is meaningfully lower. For a cabinet build-out, plan your vertical stacks within one size and use the size variation horizontally across your shelf rather than vertically in a single column.
