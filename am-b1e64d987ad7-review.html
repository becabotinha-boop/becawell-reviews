Frequently Asked Questions

How do I know which of the 9 sizes to order?

Measure your calf circumference at its widest point with a soft measuring tape, then cross-reference with the shoe size chart on the product listing. Most sizing errors happen when people use shoe size only. The calf measurement is the more important variable — if you're between two sizes and have wider calves, size up. If you're between sizes with narrower calves, size down. The listing includes a detailed sizing guide with both measurements.

Are these medical grade? Will they help with varicose veins or edema?

No — these are general-use compression socks in the 15–20 mmHg range, which is appropriate for everyday comfort and fatigue reduction. Medical-grade compression for diagnosed conditions like chronic venous insufficiency, edema, or varicose veins requires certified 20–30+ mmHg products prescribed and fitted by a healthcare provider. If you have a circulatory diagnosis, these are not a substitute for that.

Can I wear these all day, including sleeping?

Wearing them during active hours — standing, walking, sitting on flights — is the intended use case, and most people wear them 8–12 hours without issue. Sleeping in compression socks is generally not recommended unless specifically directed by a doctor, because circulation behaves differently when you're horizontal and continuous compression overnight can occasionally cause discomfort or skin irritation. For daytime use, they're designed to be worn a full workday.

How many wash cycles before they lose compression?

Based on extended use, the compression feels consistent through 40+ machine wash cycles with cold water and air drying. Machine drying on high heat is the fastest way to degrade the elastic fibers in any compression sock — this brand is no exception. If you machine dry on high, expect noticeable loosening within 20–25 cycles. Air dry or low-heat tumble dry and they last considerably longer.

Do these work for travel and long flights?

Yes — flight swelling is one of the most common reasons people try compression socks, and the 15–20 mmHg range is what most travel health guidelines recommend for economy passengers on flights over four hours. Put them on before boarding, not after your feet are already swollen. The graduated compression helps maintain circulation during extended sitting. They're thin enough to fit inside most travel shoes and don't require removal through security.
